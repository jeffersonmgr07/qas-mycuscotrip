Frequently asked questions about trains to Machu Picchu

Find useful information to book train tickets to Machu Picchu with PeruRail and Inca Rail, choose routes from Cusco, Ollantaytambo, Urubamba or Hidroelectrica, compare schedules, and plan your visit with personalized assistance from My Cusco Trip.

Where do trains to Machu Picchu depart from?

Tourist trains to Machu Picchu may depart from Ollantaytambo, Cusco, Urubamba or Hidroelectrica, depending on the railway company, season and available schedules. The most commonly used route is Ollantaytambo - Machu Picchu because of its frequency, travel time and connection with the Sacred Valley.

Which companies operate trains to Machu Picchu?

The main railway companies are PeruRail and Inca Rail. Both offer different service categories, schedules, departure stations and onboard experiences. With My Cusco Trip, you can compare available options and choose the most convenient alternative for your itinerary.

What is the best train route to Machu Picchu?

For most travelers, Ollantaytambo - Machu Picchu is the most practical route because it works well with Sacred Valley tours and offers frequent departures. Departing from Cusco can be more convenient if you prefer to leave directly from the city, although the journey is usually longer.

How far in advance should I buy my train ticket?

It is best to book your train tickets as soon as your Machu Picchu visit date is confirmed, especially during high season, holidays, weekends or periods of high demand. The most convenient schedules usually sell out first.

Can I book round-trip trains for the same day?

Yes, you can book same-day outbound and return trains as long as the schedules allow enough time for the visit. Make sure to consider the bus, entrance time, route inside Machu Picchu and the return to the station before choosing your return train.

How much time should I stay in Machu Picchu before returning?

We recommend leaving several hours between arrival in Machu Picchu Pueblo and the return train. If the return train is too close to your arrival time, the system may show an alert so you can review your schedule before booking.

What is the difference between Expedition, Vistadome and Hiram Bingham?

Expedition is a practical tourist option; Vistadome offers panoramic windows and a more scenic experience; Hiram Bingham is a luxury train with more exclusive service. Benefits and availability may vary by route, schedule and railway company conditions.

What is the difference between The Voyager, The Prime, The 360° and The First Class?

The Voyager is a functional option with cultural value; The Prime offers more comfort and onboard details; The 360° focuses on panoramic views and a visual experience; The First Class provides a more exclusive premium experience, depending on availability and route.

Does the train ticket include entrance to Machu Picchu?

In general, the train ticket does not include the Machu Picchu entrance, Consetur bus or guide, unless a specific package or service clearly states it. On this page, you can add useful extras to complement your visit and travel with better organization.

What information is needed to book train tickets?

Passenger full names, document type and number, nationality, birth date and contact details are usually required to book train tickets. This information is needed to validate ticket issuance and avoid issues on the travel date.
